Who gives a crap

The toilet paper company that is changing the world, one wipe at a time. See how this company is donating 50% of its profits and fighting a serious worldwide challenge.

2 mins

Share

Save

Who are "who gives a crap"?

Who gives a crap is a profound start-up created by Australian trio Jehan Ratnatung, Danny Alexander and Simon Giffiths. Born in 2010 through a viral IndieGogo campaign, where Simon Griffiths pledged to sit on the toilet until the fundraising minimum had been reached. 50 hours later, the "Who gives a crap" journey began.

In an interview with SmartCompany, Griffith stated that he estimated they "ended up getting the equivalent of about one million in PR spend thanks to the stunt". It goes without saying, the brand took off. Now, the modern toilet paper brand has donated $5.4 million to a variety of organisations around the world and inspired others such as "No.2" to follow suit on the initiative.

Brand mission

"Our goal is to make sure everyone has access to a toilet by 2050. Building a sustainable business model that delivers quality and environmentally responsible products at competitive prices is how we’re going to get there.”

The brand operates on a millennial style subscription-based web purchase, sold direct-to-consumer model. Making what could quite frankly be the most boring product out there, a fun and attractive experience for young people in particular. You could even call it trendy. Granting "who gives a crap" relentless traction from the get-go.

Donating 50% of profits:

Who gives a crap has explained who assesses their claim to be donating 50% of profits generated in the following statement:

“We are certified B Corp, which means that we’ve been assessed as a ‘for benefit’ company, rather than purely ‘for profit’. This certification covers a wide range of aspects of our business and ranks our performance across a rigorous set of criteria. Part of this certification involved an independent assessment of our financial statements confirming our claim to donate at least 50% to charities.”

B Corporations are a group of companies that are using the power of business to create a positive impact on the world and generate a shared and durable prosperity for all. The Who Gives A Crap 2016 B Impact Report, which is published online, gives Who Gives A Crap a score of 101, out of a maximum of 200, where as the median score for all assessed companies is just 55.
